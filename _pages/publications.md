My research is inspired by environmental photochemical reactions that cannot be explained by our current understanding of chemistry. In my research career, I have used an interdisciplinary approach that includes theoretical and computational chemistry, experimental photochemistry, and field work, to study these photochemical reactions. In the face of climate change, an interdisciplinary approach is necessary to increase our chemical knowledge of natural global cycles. 

In my research, I have focused on understanding unexplainable dramatic photochemical isotope effects of mercury complexes in natural waters and within plankton.
